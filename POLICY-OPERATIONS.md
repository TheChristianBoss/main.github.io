# Policy operations checklist

The public policy pages describe the website's current implementation and establish operating commitments. Keep the actual practices consistent with the pages.

## Applicant records

- Keep job applications only in the restricted Discord channel used for hiring.
- Limit access to people directly involved in hiring or administration.
- Delete an application no later than 12 months after the role is filled, closed, or a decision is made unless a documented exception applies.
- Process access, correction, withdrawal, and deletion requests sent to `hello@christiangoblin.com`.
- Do not repost applicant content into public or general community channels.

## Store and service records

- Keep payment-card details in Stripe; never copy full card data into Discord, GitHub, email, or notes.
- Keep operational, tax, payment, chargeback, and accounting records no longer than seven years unless a different period is required.
- Review service-order Discord content and remove unnecessary sensitive details.
- Keep `STORE_ORDER_KV` configured; its fulfillment records normally expire after 120 days.
- Respond to damaged or incorrect physical-item reports received within 30 days after delivery.

## Contact and support records

- Review general contact/support records after 24 months of inactivity and delete them when no longer needed.
- Never request passwords or private keys through public forms or ordinary email.

## Policy maintenance

Review the policy pages whenever any of these change:

- contact email or operator identity;
- hosting, payment, fulfillment, CAPTCHA, Discord, or analytics providers;
- application fields or hiring workflow;
- retention periods;
- products, services, refund rules, or delivery promises;
- whether files are processed locally or uploaded to a server.

Update the effective date whenever a material change is published.

## Legal review

These pages are an implementation-based policy draft, not a substitute for advice from a qualified attorney. A legal review is especially important before hiring employees, collecting information from minors, offering regulated professional services, or selling broadly outside the United States.
